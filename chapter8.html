Chapter 8

Computer Security

NOTES

The chapter begins with Computer Security, addressing the various threats and protective measures. Key topics include: Computer Threats: Identifying common threats like viruses, worms, Trojan horses, spyware, adware, and ransomware. It also covers phishing, spamming, and hacking. Security Measures: Discussing methods to protect data and systems, such as using antivirus software, firewalls, strong passwords, data encryption, and regular backups. Data Security: Concepts related to safeguarding personal and sensitive information. Next, it delves into Computer Ethics, outlining the moral principles that govern computer use. This includes: Ethical Issues: Discussing topics like cyberbullying, plagiarism, digital privacy, and intellectual property rights (e.g., copyright, software piracy).
